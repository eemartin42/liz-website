Featured Research

My research focuses on understanding mechanisms of drug resistance in cancer, with particular emphasis on targeted therapies and antibody-drug conjugates. I use computational approaches to develop pipelines, analyze genomic and transcriptomic data, and use statistical methods to identify resistance mechanisms. My projects have focused on analyzing tumor heterogeneity and tumor evolution using data from patients who have undergone rapid autopsies at time of death. Below are selected publications that highlight what we have discovered using this unique data type. My contributions include project ideation, computational analysis, methods development, and writing.

Parallel Mechanisms of Resistance to Sacituzumab Goveitecan in Triple-Negative Breast Cancer

Using rapid autopsy lesions, we reconstructed the phylogeny of a patient who had been treated with the antibody-drug conjugate sacituzumab goveitecan. We found that the patient had developed resistance to both the target and the payload of the drug in two different branches of the phylogeneic tree, indicating independent mechanisms of resistance.

Breast Cancer Antibody-Drug Conjugates
View Paper

On-Target Resistance to PI3K Inhibitors via Secondary Mutations

Rapid autopsy cases showed that patients with clonal PIK3CA mutations developed subclonal second hits in PIK3CA as well as other genes in the PI3K pathway along multiple branches of the phylogenetic tree that conferred resistance to PI3Ki, showing cancer evolution in response to the drug. We show that these mutations can be overcome by a novel allosteric PI3K inhibitor.

Breast Cancer PI3K Inhibitors
View Paper

Understanding Acquired Resistance to FGFR2i in Cholangiocarcinoma

In this project, we used several rapid autopsy cases to characterize the spectrum of FGFR2 mutations observed in patients treated with a covalent FGFR2 inhibitor.

Cholangiocarcinoma FGFR2 Inhibitors
